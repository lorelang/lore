<domain_ontology>
<overview>
Domain: b2b-saas-gtm
Description: Domain ontology for a B2B SaaS company's go-to-market operations. Models accounts, contacts, subscriptions, product usage, and engagement signals to power an AI agent that identifies revenue expansion opportunities, predicts churn risk, and recommends GTM plays.

Scoped to view: Account Executive
Audience: Account Executives, Sales Leadership
View purpose: Scoped perspective for Account Executives focused on expansion pipeline, deal progression, and revenue growth.

</overview>

<entities>
<entity name="Account">
  A company that is a customer, prospect, or former customer. Accounts are the primary commercial unit — all revenue, contracts, and engagement roll up to the account level.


  Attributes:
    - name: string [required, unique]
      Legal entity name or commonly used company name.
    - domain: string [unique]
      Primary web domain (e.g., acme.com). Used for identity resolution and enrichment.
    - industry: string
      Industry vertical per our ICP definition.
    - industry_seasonality: enum [cyclical, stable, unknown]
      Whether this industry has predictable seasonal patterns that affect usage. Cyclical industries (retail, travel, construction) show usage dips that are NOT churn signals. The usage-decline-alert rule should adjust thresholds for cyclical accounts. See outcomes/q2-retrospective.lore for the false-positive analysis that motivated this field.
    - fiscal_year_end: enum [q1, q2, q3, q4, unknown]
      When this account's fiscal year ends. Affects budget cycle timing — expansion pitches should land 2-3 months before fiscal year end when budget is being allocated.
    - employee_count: int
      Most recent headcount from enrichment data.
    - segment: enum [enterprise, mid-market, smb]
      Determined by ARR band. Enterprise >= $100K ARR, Mid-market $25K-$100K, SMB < $25K.
    - arr: float
      Current annualized recurring revenue from all active subscriptions. Updated on each billing cycle.
    - stage: enum [prospect, onboarding, active, at-risk, churned, former]
      Current lifecycle stage. See Lifecycle section.
    - health_score: float [0.0 .. 100.0]
      Composite score reflecting engagement, usage, support sentiment, and commercial signals.
    - nrr_trailing_12m: float
      Net revenue retention for this account over trailing 12 months. Values above 1.0 indicate expansion; below 1.0 indicate contraction.
    - csm_owner: reference to Contact
      The CSM responsible for this account. References our internal team member (modeled as Contact with is_internal = true).
    - ae_owner: reference to Contact
      The Account Executive who owns the commercial relationship.
    - icp_fit_score: float [0.0 .. 1.0]
      How closely this account matches our Ideal Customer Profile. Based on industry, size, tech stack, and use case alignment.

  Identity: An account is uniquely identified by its primary web domain.
If two business entities share a domain, they are treated as
one account. Parent/subsidiary relationships are modeled via
the SUBSIDIARY_OF relationship, not as separate domain entries.

  Lifecycle: Accounts progress through stages based on commercial and
engagement milestones:

- **Prospect**: No signed contract. May have open opportunities.
- **Onboarding**: First contract signed, implementation in progress.
  Transition to Active requires: (1) product deployed in production,
  (2) at least 5 active users, (3) first value milestone confirmed.
- **Active**: Live customer with healthy engagement.
- **At-risk**: Health score dropped below 40 OR NRR < 0.85 OR
  champion departed. Triggers CSM intervention playbook.
- **Churned**: All subscriptions cancelled or expired. Grace period
  of 30 days before moving to Former.
- **Former**: Previously churned, now a win-back target.

  Notes: Account data is enriched weekly from Clearbit/ZoomInfo.
Revenue data syncs daily from the billing system (Stripe).
Stage transitions are automated by rules/scoring.lore but
can be manually overridden by CSM with a logged reason.

**Expected adoption curves by segment:**
- SMB: should reach adoption_score > 0.5 within 30 days.
  If not, the product is likely too complex for their team
  size. Recommend training or a simplified onboarding flow.
- Mid-market: 60-day window to reach 0.5 adoption. These
  accounts benefit most from implementation services.
- Enterprise: 90-day window is normal. Complex onboarding
  with multiple departments, IT review, SSO setup. Do not
  trigger adoption concern signals before 90 days.

**Budget cycle intelligence:**
- Expansion pitches land best 2-3 months before fiscal year
  end (when budget is being planned) or in Q1 of their fiscal
  year (when new budget is available).
- Avoid expansion conversations in the last month of their
  fiscal year — budget is already committed.
- If fiscal_year_end is unknown, assume Q4 (calendar year)
  unless the account is in government (Q3) or education (Q2).

**Cohort context:**
- Always compare an account's metrics to its cohort (same
  segment, similar onboarding date). An enterprise account
  with 0.4 adoption at 60 days is on track. An SMB account
  with 0.4 adoption at 60 days is falling behind.
- Peer benchmarks: the agent should know what "good" looks
  like for this account's segment and industry.

</entity>

<entity name="Contact">
  A person associated with an account. Can be an external stakeholder (customer) or internal team member. Contacts are the human nodes in the relationship graph — deals, engagement, and influence flow through people.


  Attributes:
    - name: string [required]
      Full name as it appears in CRM.
    - email: string [required, unique]
      Primary email address. Used for identity resolution. @pii
    - title: string
      Current job title.
    - role: enum [champion, economic-buyer, technical-buyer, end-user, executive-sponsor, blocker, unknown]
      Functional role in the buying/retention process. Champion: internal advocate who drives adoption. Economic buyer: controls budget and signs contracts. Blocker: actively resists expansion or renewal.
    - department: string
      Organizational department (Engineering, Marketing, etc.)
    - seniority: enum [ic, manager, director, vp, c-level]
      Level in org hierarchy. Affects influence weight.
    - is_internal: boolean
      True if this contact is our employee (CSM, AE, SE). Internal contacts are modeled as Contacts so they can participate in relationships like OWNS and ENGAGED_WITH.
    - last_engaged: datetime
      Timestamp of most recent interaction (email, meeting, login).
    - engagement_score: float [0.0 .. 100.0]
      Composite score based on recency, frequency, and depth of interactions.
    - champion_strength: float [0.0 .. 1.0]
      For contacts with role = champion, how strong is their advocacy? Based on internal mentions, feature requests, reference willingness, executive access.

  Identity: A contact is uniquely identified by email address.
If a person has multiple email addresses, the primary
(work) email is canonical. Personal emails are stored
as aliases but do not create separate contact records.

  Notes: Contact enrichment runs via Clearbit on creation and
monthly thereafter. Title and seniority changes are
tracked — a champion who gets promoted is a strong
expansion signal; a champion who leaves is a churn risk.

</entity>

<entity name="Opportunity">
  A potential revenue event — new business, expansion, or renewal. Opportunities track the commercial pipeline and are the unit of GTM forecasting.


  Attributes:
    - name: string [required]
      Descriptive name (e.g., "Acme Corp - Enterprise Upgrade Q2").
    - type: enum [new-business, expansion, renewal, win-back]
      The commercial motion this opportunity represents.
    - stage: enum [discovery, qualification, evaluation, negotiation, closed-won, closed-lost]
      Current pipeline stage.
    - amount: float
      Projected ARR value if closed-won.
    - close_date: date
      Expected close date.
    - probability: float [0.0 .. 1.0]
      Win probability based on stage and engagement signals.
    - source: enum [csm-identified, agent-recommended, ae-created, product-led, inbound]
      How this opportunity was generated. Agent-recommended means the AI expansion agent identified and proposed it.
    - products: reference to Product
      Which products are included in this opportunity.
    - primary_contact: reference to Contact
      Main point of contact for this deal.
    - champion: reference to Contact [optional]
      Internal champion advocating for this deal.
    - blockers: list<string>
      Known obstacles to closing (budget freeze, competing priority, technical concern, etc.)

  Notes: Expansion opportunities are the primary output of the
revenue expansion agent. When the agent identifies an
expansion signal, it creates an opportunity recommendation
with pre-populated fields based on the signal analysis.

An opportunity without an identified champion has a
historically 3x lower close rate and should be flagged.

</entity>

<entity name="Product">
  Status: stable
  Provenance: source=domain-expert, confidence=0.95, author=product-team, created=2025-01-15
  A product or plan that we sell. Products are the units of our catalog — accounts subscribe to products, and expansion happens when accounts add new products or upgrade existing ones. See glossary/products.lore for canonical definitions and taxonomies/product-catalog.lore for the full hierarchy.


  Attributes:
    - name: string [required, unique]
      Product name as it appears in pricing page and contracts.
    - sku: string [required, unique]
      Internal product identifier.
    - tier: enum [starter, professional, enterprise]
      Pricing tier. Determines feature set and limits. See taxonomies/product-catalog.lore for full tier details.
    - base_price_monthly: float
      List price per seat per month before discounts. Enterprise is annual-only: multiply by 12 × seat minimum.
    - pricing_model: enum [per-seat, flat-fee, one-time]
      How this product is billed. Core tiers are per-seat. Data Export is flat-fee. Services are one-time.
    - minimum_seats: int [optional]
      Minimum seat count for this product. Enterprise = 50. Below this, the account should be on a lower tier.
    - category: enum [core-platform, add-on, professional-services]
      Core platform is the main product. Add-ons extend it. Professional services are implementation/consulting.
    - available_with: list<string> [optional]
      Which tiers this product can be attached to. E.g., Analytics add-on: ["professional", "enterprise"]. Empty means available to all tiers.
    - features: list<string>
      Key feature slugs included in this product. Maps to Feature.slug for adoption tracking and whitespace analysis.
    - upgrade_path: reference to Product [optional]
      The natural next product for accounts on this plan. Starter -> Professional -> Enterprise.
    - typical_sales_cycle_days: int [optional]
      Average days from opportunity creation to close. Starter: 1 (self-serve), Professional: 14, Enterprise: 45.
    - attach_rate: float [0.0 .. 1.0] [optional]
      Current penetration for add-on products within their eligible base. Updated quarterly by RevOps.

  Identity: Products are uniquely identified by SKU. Product names can
change (marketing rebranding) but SKUs are permanent.

  Notes: Expansion revenue can come from three motions:
1. **Seat expansion** (more users on same plan) — frictionless,
   often doesn't need AE involvement. Triggered by seat
   utilization > 85%.
2. **Tier upgrade** (Starter -> Professional -> Enterprise) —
   highest ACV impact but requires champion + business case.
   Triggered by feature ceiling, security requirements, or
   user count exceeding tier limits.
3. **Add-on attach** (Analytics or Data Export added) —
   medium ACV, targeted pitch. Triggered by usage patterns
   that indicate the account is doing manually what the
   add-on automates.

The agent should understand which motion fits each account
and recommend the right play. A Professional account with
50+ users and security requests is an Enterprise upgrade
candidate. A Professional account that runs 30 reports
a month is an Analytics attach candidate. Both are
"expansion" but the play is completely different.

Professional services are not recurring revenue but they
are a leading indicator of retention and expansion. The
agent should recommend services alongside product sales —
an Enterprise upgrade without implementation services
leads to 40% lower adoption at 90 days.

</entity>

<entity name="Signal">
  A detected event or pattern that indicates a change in account trajectory — either toward expansion or toward churn. Signals are the AI agent's primary input for generating recommendations.


  Attributes:
    - type: reference to SignalType
      Classification of the signal per taxonomies/signal-types.lore
    - strength: enum [strong, moderate, weak]
      Confidence level based on signal clarity and historical predictive value.
    - direction: enum [expansion, contraction, neutral]
      Whether this signal suggests growth or risk.
    - detected_at: datetime [required]
      When the signal was first detected.
    - source_data: text
      Raw evidence that generated this signal. Could be a usage metric change, an email excerpt, a support ticket, or a product event.
    - status: enum [new, acknowledged, actioned, dismissed]
      Workflow status. New signals need review; actioned means a play has been triggered.
    - recommended_play: string [optional]
      The GTM play the agent recommends based on this signal. References a playbook entry.

  Notes: Signals are ephemeral — they represent a point-in-time
observation. Multiple related signals may be clustered
into a "signal group" that tells a coherent story. For
example: usage spike + new department onboarded + executive
meeting requested = strong expansion signal cluster.

Signal detection runs daily. Historical signals older than
90 days that were never actioned are auto-archived.

</entity>

</entities>

<relationships>
  Account -[HAS_SUBSCRIPTION]-> Subscription (one-to-many)
    An account can have multiple subscriptions (multi-product). All subscriptions roll up to the account's total ARR.
  Account -[HAS_CONTACT]-> Contact (one-to-many)
    Contacts belong to accounts. A contact can only belong to one account (if they move companies, a new contact record is created and linked via PREVIOUSLY_AT).
  Subscription -[SUBSCRIBES_TO]-> Product (many-to-one)
    Each subscription is for one product/plan. An account with multiple products has multiple subscriptions.
  Account -[HAS_OPPORTUNITY]-> Opportunity (one-to-many)
    Opportunities are attached to accounts. An account can have multiple open opportunities simultaneously.
  Contact -[OWNS]-> Account (one-to-many)
    Internal contacts (CSMs, AEs) own accounts. One person can own many accounts; an account has one CSM and one AE.
    Properties: role: enum [csm, ae, se, executive-sponsor], since: date
  Account -[SUBSIDIARY_OF]-> Account (many-to-one)
    Parent/subsidiary relationship. Revenue may be reported at parent level for enterprise rollups.
  Contact -[PARTICIPATED_IN]-> Interaction (many-to-many)
    Contacts participate in interactions. Both internal (our team) and external (customer) contacts are linked.
    Properties: role: enum [organizer, attendee, sender, recipient]
  Account -[HAS_USAGE]-> Usage (one-to-many)
    Usage records are per account per period. An account accumulates usage records over time forming a time series.
  Account -[TRIGGERED]-> Signal (one-to-many)
    Signals are detected at the account level. An account can have many active signals simultaneously.
  Signal -[GENERATED_FROM]-> Interaction (many-to-one [optional])
    Some signals originate from specific interactions (e.g., a negative sentiment support ticket generates a churn signal). Links signal to its evidence.
  Signal -[GENERATED_FROM_USAGE]-> Usage (many-to-one [optional])
    Some signals originate from usage patterns (e.g., usage spike generates an expansion signal).
  Signal -[RESULTED_IN]-> Opportunity (many-to-one [optional])
    When a signal is actioned and an opportunity is created, this links the signal to the outcome. Used for measuring signal-to-pipeline conversion.
  Product -[INCLUDES_FEATURE]-> Feature (many-to-many)
    Products include features. A feature can appear in multiple products (e.g., basic-reporting is in all tiers). The agent uses this to map what an account has access to versus what they're actually using.
  Account -[HAS_ADOPTED]-> Feature (many-to-many)
    Tracks which features an account is actively using. "Adopted" means the feature was used by 2+ users in the last 30 days. The gap between INCLUDES_FEATURE (what they have) and HAS_ADOPTED (what they use) is the adoption gap. When any user at this account first used this feature. Users who used this feature in the last 30 days. Trial: used 1-2 times. Light: weekly. Moderate: daily by some users. Heavy: daily by most users.
    Properties: first_used: date, monthly_active_users: int, adoption_depth: enum [trial, light, moderate, heavy]
  Competitor -[COMPETES_WITH]-> Product (many-to-many)
    Which of our products a competitor directly challenges. CompeteFlow competes with Professional. DataStack competes with Enterprise. "Spreadsheets" competes with Starter. What specifically overlaps (e.g., "workflow automation", "enterprise security", "basic project management").
    Properties: overlap_area: string
  Opportunity -[LOST_TO]-> Competitor (many-to-one [optional])
    When a deal is closed-lost to a specific competitor. Critical for tracking competitive win/loss rates and understanding where we're weak. Primary reason for the loss (price, feature gap, etc.)
    Properties: loss_reason: string
  Signal -[RECOMMENDS_PLAY]-> Play (many-to-one [optional])
    Links a detected signal to the recommended play. This replaces the free-text recommended_play field with a structured reference to the Play entity.
  Feature -[REQUIRES_FEATURE]-> Feature (many-to-one [optional])
    Feature prerequisite chain. Cohort analysis requires advanced analytics. Conditional logic requires workflow automation. The agent uses this to understand adoption sequence — don't recommend an advanced feature if the prerequisite hasn't been adopted yet.
</relationships>

<traversals>
These are valid multi-hop reasoning paths in this domain:

  revenue-by-product:
    Path: Account -[HAS_SUBSCRIPTION]-> Subscription -[SUBSCRIBES_TO]-> Product
    Use: Answers: "What products does this account use and what's the revenue for each?"

  account-portfolio:
    Path: Contact -[OWNS]-> Account -[HAS_SUBSCRIPTION]-> Subscription
    Use: Answers: "What's the total book of business for this CSM/AE?" Used for workload balancing and quota tracking.

  expansion-pipeline:
    Path: Account -[HAS_OPPORTUNITY]-> Opportunity -[type = expansion]
    Use: Answers: "What expansion deals are in flight for this account or across the portfolio?"

  parent-rollup:
    Path: Account -[SUBSIDIARY_OF]-> Account (recursive)
    Use: Answers: "What's the total ARR across the entire corporate hierarchy?" Important for enterprise pricing and executive engagement strategy.

  signal-to-evidence:
    Path: Signal -[GENERATED_FROM]-> Interaction -[PARTICIPATED_IN]- Contact
    Use: Answers: "What happened that triggered this signal, and who was involved?" Essential for CSM context before taking action on a signal.

  account-engagement-timeline:
    Path: Account -[HAS_CONTACT]-> Contact -[PARTICIPATED_IN]-> Interaction
    Use: Answers: "What's the full engagement history for this account?" Ordered by timestamp, gives a narrative view of the relationship.

  signal-to-revenue:
    Path: Signal -[RESULTED_IN]-> Opportunity -[stage = closed-won]
    Use: Answers: "How much pipeline and revenue have our signals generated?" Key metric for proving AI agent ROI.

  feature-adoption-gap:
    Path: Account -[HAS_SUBSCRIPTION]-> Subscription -[SUBSCRIBES_TO]-> Product -[INCLUDES_FEATURE]-> Feature
    Use: Answers: "What features does this account have access to?" Compare against Account -[HAS_ADOPTED]-> Feature to find the adoption gap — features available but not used. Large adoption gaps in "aha moment" features are intervention opportunities (training, CSM check-in).

  competitive-exposure:
    Path: Account -[HAS_SUBSCRIPTION]-> Subscription -[SUBSCRIBES_TO]-> Product -[COMPETES_WITH]- Competitor
    Use: Answers: "Which competitors are relevant for this account based on what products they use?" When a competitor-threat signal fires, this traversal identifies which competitor and pulls the counter-play.

  signal-to-play:
    Path: Signal -[RECOMMENDS_PLAY]-> Play
    Use: Answers: "What should the CSM/AE do in response to this signal?" Direct link from detection to action.

  win-loss-by-competitor:
    Path: Opportunity -[LOST_TO]-> Competitor
    Use: Answers: "What's our win/loss rate against each competitor?" Aggregated to update Competitor.win_rate_against quarterly.

  feature-upgrade-path:
    Path: Feature -[REQUIRES_FEATURE]-> Feature
    Use: Answers: "What's the natural adoption sequence for this account?" Used by the agent to recommend the NEXT feature to adopt, not skip ahead.

</traversals>

<rules>
<rule name="champion-departure-alert">
  Applies to: Account
  Severity: critical
  Trigger: Contact with role=champion becomes inactive or leaves
  Condition: Contact WHERE account = this
AND role = "champion"
AND (last_engaged < now() - 60d OR title changed)
  Action: Create Signal with type = "Champion Departure"
Set signal.strength = "strong"
Set signal.direction = "contraction"
Set account.stage = "at-risk" IF health_score < 60
Notify csm_owner immediately
  Context: Champion departure is historically our #1 churn predictor.
  When detected, the CSM should:
  1. Confirm whether the champion has left or changed roles
  2. If left: identify and cultivate a new champion within 14 days
  3. If changed roles: assess whether new role still touches product
  4. Map the remaining stakeholder landscape — is there anyone
     else who can advocate for renewal?

  If no replacement champion is identified within 30 days,
  escalate to CSM manager for executive intervention.
</rule>

<rule name="usage-decline-alert">
  Applies to: Account
  Severity: warning
  Trigger: Usage trend shifts to declining
  Condition: usage.usage_trend = "declining"
AND usage.active_users < 0.7 * avg(usage.active_users, last 3 months)
  Action: Create Signal with type = "Usage Decline"
Set signal.strength based on decline severity:
- >40% decline: "strong"
- 20-40% decline: "moderate"
Recommended play: "Value realization check-in — don't
lead with 'we noticed your usage dropped.' Instead ask
about their current priorities and whether the product
is aligned with what they need right now."
</rule>

<rule name="competitor-threat">
  Applies to: Account
  Severity: critical
  Trigger: Competitor mentioned in interaction
  Condition: Interaction WHERE account = this
AND topics includes any of ["competitor", known_competitor_names]
AND sentiment IN ["negative", "neutral"]
AND timestamp > now() - 30d
  Action: Create Signal with type = "Competitor Mention"
Set signal.strength = "strong"
Set signal.direction = "contraction"
Notify csm_owner AND ae_owner immediately
  Context: Competitor mentions combined with negative sentiment are
  an acute threat. The response must be fast and strategic —
  not defensive. The play is to understand what gap the
  competitor appears to fill and address it directly.
</rule>

<rule name="renewal-risk-scoring">
  Applies to: Subscription
  Severity: warning
  Trigger: Subscription within 90 days of renewal
  Condition: subscription.renewal_date < now() + 90d
AND subscription.status = "active"
Evaluate renewal risk based on composite factors:
- Seat utilization < 0.6 → high contraction risk
- Health score < 40 → high churn risk
- No champion identified → elevated risk
- No executive engagement in 90+ days → elevated risk
- NRR trailing 12m < 0.9 → prior contraction pattern
  Action: Calculate composite renewal risk score
If high risk: escalate to csm_owner + manager
If medium risk: add to CSM priority queue
Recommended play depends on primary risk factor
</rule>

<rule name="multi-signal-expansion">
  Applies to: Account
  Severity: info
  Trigger: Multiple expansion signals detected within 30 days
  Condition: count(Signal WHERE account = this
AND direction = "expansion"
AND strength IN ["strong", "moderate"]
AND detected_at > now() - 30d) >= 2
  Action: Create Opportunity with type = "expansion"
Set opportunity.source = "agent-recommended"
Calculate recommended products via whitespace-analysis
Notify ae_owner with signal summary
  Context: When multiple expansion signals cluster within a short
  window, it indicates a genuine shift in account trajectory,
  not just noise. The agent should synthesize the signals
  into a narrative: "Acme has added 15 new users from the
  marketing team this month, hit their API rate limit twice,
  and their VP of Engineering attended our webinar. This
  suggests cross-departmental adoption momentum."
</rule>

<rule name="whitespace-analysis">
  Applies to: Account
  Severity: info
  Condition: account.stage = "active"
AND account.health_score > 60
For each product in our catalog that the account does NOT
currently subscribe to, evaluate fit based on:
1. Are they using features that are precursors to this product?
(e.g., using basic reporting heavily -> analytics add-on)
2. Does their industry/segment typically adopt this product?
3. Have similar accounts expanded into this product?
4. Is there usage evidence of the need? (e.g., exporting
data to do externally what our add-on does natively)
  Action: Attach whitespace analysis to account record
Rank products by fit score
If top product fit > 0.7, create expansion signal
</rule>

<rule name="seat-expansion-trigger">
  Applies to: Subscription
  Severity: info
  Trigger: Seat utilization exceeds threshold
  Condition: subscription.seat_utilization > 0.85
AND subscription.status = "active"
  Action: Create Signal with type = "Feature Ceiling Hit"
Set signal.strength = "strong"
Set signal.direction = "expansion"
Recommended play: "Proactive seat expansion offer —
reach out before they hit the limit. Offer volume
discount for committing to higher seat count."
</rule>

<rule name="tier-upgrade-readiness">
  Applies to: Account
  Severity: info
  Trigger: Account shows pattern of needing next-tier features
  Condition: account.stage = "active"
AND subscription.plan.tier IN ["starter", "professional"]
AND usage.adoption_score > 0.8
AND usage.usage_trend = "growing"
AND account.health_score > 70
The account is getting maximum value from their current tier
and showing growth signals. They are likely hitting limitations
that the next tier would resolve.
  Action: Create Signal with type = "Feature Ceiling Hit"
Set signal.direction = "expansion"
Recommended play: "Tier upgrade conversation — frame
around unlocking value, not increasing spend. Lead with
the 2-3 features they'd gain that map to their usage
patterns."
</rule>

<rule name="account-health-score">
  Applies to: Account
  Severity: info
  Computes: Account.health_score
  Context: The health score is a weighted composite of four dimensions:

  - **Product adoption** (30%): Based on usage.adoption_score
    averaged over last 3 months. Higher adoption = getting
    more value = healthier.

  - **Engagement quality** (25%): Based on interaction recency,
    frequency, and sentiment. Weighted toward recent interactions.
    Executive engagement counts 2x.

  - **Support health** (20%): Inverse of open ticket count and
    average resolution time. Accounts with many open negative
    tickets score low.

  - **Commercial signals** (25%): Seat utilization, NRR trend,
    on-time payments. An expanding account scores high; a
    contracting one scores low.

  Scores are recalculated daily. A drop of more than 15 points
  in a single week triggers a CSM alert regardless of absolute score.
</rule>

<rule name="contact-engagement-score">
  Applies to: Contact
  Severity: info
  Computes: Contact.engagement_score
  Context: Based on the contact's interaction history:
  - Recency: When was their last interaction? (exponential decay)
  - Frequency: How often do they engage per month?
  - Depth: Are they just attending meetings or actively driving
    discussions (organizer vs attendee, sender vs recipient)?
  - Trend: Is engagement increasing or decreasing?

  A contact with high engagement_score who suddenly drops is
  a churn signal. A contact with increasing engagement who
  has seniority >= director is an expansion signal.
</rule>

<rule name="champion-strength">
  Applies to: Contact
  Severity: info
  Computes: Contact.champion_strength
  Context: Only computed for contacts with role = "champion".
  Based on:
  - Internal advocacy actions (feature requests, case study
    participation, reference calls)
  - Executive access (can they get us meetings with VPs/C-level?)
  - Adoption driving (did usage increase in their department?)
  - Public advocacy (social mentions, conference talks, reviews)

  A champion_strength > 0.8 is a "power champion" — these
  accounts almost never churn. Below 0.3 means the champion
  designation should be re-evaluated.
</rule>

<rule name="adoption-score">
  Applies to: Usage
  Severity: info
  Computes: Usage.adoption_score
  Context: Calculated as: count(features_used) / count(features_entitled)

  Not all features are weighted equally. "Aha moment" features
  (identified from historical activation analysis) count 3x:
  - Workflow automation
  - Custom dashboards
  - API integration
  - Team collaboration features
</rule>

<rule name="usage-trend">
  Applies to: Usage
  Severity: info
  Computes: Usage.usage_trend
  Context: Compare current period's active_users against the average
  of the prior 3 periods:
  - Growing: current > avg * 1.1 (>10% increase)
  - Declining: current < avg * 0.8 (<20% decrease)
  - Stable: everything else
</rule>

<glossary>
Canonical definitions — use these when interpreting user queries:

  ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue): The annualized value of all active subscriptions for an
account. Calculated as monthly recurring revenue × 12.
Excludes one-time fees, professional services, and
overages. ARR is our primary revenue metric.

Do NOT confuse with ACV (Annual Contract Value), which
includes one-time fees and is used for deal sizing.

  NRR (Net Revenue Retention): Trailing 12-month revenue from existing accounts divided
by the revenue those same accounts had 12 months ago.
Includes expansion, contraction, and churn.
NRR > 100% means existing accounts are growing net of churn.

Formula: (Starting ARR + Expansion - Contraction - Churn) / Starting ARR

Do NOT confuse with GRR (Gross Revenue Retention), which
excludes expansion and only measures contraction + churn.

  Expansion Revenue: Net new ARR from existing customers. Sources include:
seat expansion, tier upgrades, and add-on product purchases.
Does NOT include price increases at renewal — those are
classified as "pricing uplift" and tracked separately.

  Contraction: Decrease in ARR from an existing account that remains a
customer. Causes include: seat reduction, tier downgrade,
or removal of an add-on product. An account can contract
without churning.

  Churn: Complete loss of an account's revenue. All subscriptions
cancelled or not renewed. Measured as churned ARR / total
ARR at start of period. Churn is calculated at the account
level, not the subscription level.

Voluntary churn: customer chose to leave.
Involuntary churn: payment failure, account closure.

  Whitespace: Products or capabilities in our catalog that a customer
does not currently subscribe to but could benefit from.
Whitespace analysis compares the account's current
subscriptions against the full product catalog, weighted
by the account's usage patterns and industry benchmarks.

  Champion: A contact at the customer who actively advocates for our
product internally. Champions drive adoption, defend budget
during cuts, and expand usage to new teams. Identified by:
high engagement score, proactive outreach, feature request
frequency, and willingness to do references.

A champion is NOT just a power user. A power user uses the
product a lot; a champion influences others to use it too.

  Economic Buyer: The person who controls the budget and signs contracts.
Often different from the champion. In enterprise accounts,
the economic buyer is typically VP+ level while the champion
may be a director or senior manager.

  ICP (Ideal Customer Profile): The firmographic and behavioral profile of accounts most
likely to become and remain successful customers. Our ICP
is defined by: industry, employee count, tech stack,
use case alignment, and growth trajectory. ICP fit score
measures how closely an account matches.

  Health Score: A composite metric (0-100) reflecting the overall health of
the customer relationship. See rules/scoring.lore#account-health-score
for the calculation methodology. Health score is a lagging
indicator — it reflects what has already happened. Signals
are the leading indicators of where health is heading.

  Signal: A detected event or pattern that predicts a change in
account trajectory. Signals are the primary input to the
expansion and churn detection engines. See
taxonomies/signal-types.lore for the full classification.

  Play / Playbook: A recommended sequence of actions in response to a signal
or account state. Plays are prescriptive — they tell the
CSM or AE what to do, who to contact, and what messaging
to use. Plays are NOT fully automated; they require human
judgment and execution.

  Book of Business: The set of accounts owned by a CSM or AE. Measured by
total ARR, account count, and weighted pipeline. Used
for workload balancing and territory planning.

</glossary>
